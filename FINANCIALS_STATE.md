## December Year-End Adjusting JE (recurring pattern)
**December always includes a year-end accountant adjusting JE** (tax-minimization mechanism, confirmed by Jay — recurs every December). It dumps real recurring costs (utilities, credit card fees, sales tax expense, contract labor, outside services — varies by year) that weren't booked monthly, all at once. **Do NOT treat December as noise/outlier to exclude — the annual total including December is the honest number.** Jan-Nov trends understate true costs since real recurring expenses are deferred to the Dec catch-up.
- 2023 JE (#6720): -$37,053 P&L impact, driven by Contract Labor $32K, Outside Services $15.5K, Utilities $6.3K (none of these appeared in any other 2023 month).
- 2024 JE (#9231): -$17,036 Dec net loss, driven by Credit Card Fees $10.6K, Sales Tax Expense $12.8K, Parking Fees/Tolls $6.6K (reclassed out of Travel Expenses).
- 2025: Dec was net **positive** ($5,757) — unusual vs. prior years; Jan-Nov 2025 alone was net negative (-$1,766), so 2025's full-year profitability actually came FROM the Dec adjustment, opposite of 2023/2024's pattern.

## NNN/DSC-Offset Mechanism (critical context, explained by Jay 2026-07-05)
In prior years, DSC took payment directly from catering-event revenue to offset NNN charges — this revenue never hit Motorburger's books, but the food/labor cost to produce those events still hit COGS/wages in full. This means:
- Booked NNN in 2023-2025 was likely understated relative to true obligation (part was paid in-kind via events, invisible in the P&L).
- Food cost % / labor % in those years were likely slightly overstated (real costs, no matching revenue credit).
- 2026's NNN ($12,406 booked in H1 alone, already exceeding any prior full year) is likely NOT a real cost increase — mostly the same obligation becoming visible for the first time as the offset mechanism isn't running (less catering to offset with).
- **Open item:** ask DSC for historical value of catering-revenue-offset-against-NNN by year, to confirm whether 2026 is a real increase or just newly transparent.

## Benchmark Context — Food Hall, NOT Standalone Restaurant Lease
- Standard "6-10% of revenue" occupancy benchmark cited everywhere online is for standalone restaurant leases and explicitly EXCLUDES utilities — does not apply directly here.
- Food hall vendor rent/revenue-share norms: **8-15% of gross sales** (high-volume anchor concepts 8-10%, specialty/lower-volume 12-15%). Total occupancy (rent+CAM) targeted **below 12-15%** as the "healthy" ceiling for food hall vendors.
- Food hall CAM legitimately bundles utilities, security, cleaning, common-area staff, marketing/events, management fee — DSC's NNN structure (linens, utilities, cleaning, porter split, security, event planner) is standard for this business model, not unusual.
- Against the correct (food hall) benchmark: 2023-2024 were fine, 2025 was at the edge, 2026 H1 pace is above ceiling — but largely explained by the Jan NNN spike + offset-visibility shift, not a steady structural climb.
- Some food halls (e.g. Malcolm Yards, Minneapolis) use a pure all-inclusive percentage-of-gross-sales license model instead of base rent + itemized NNN — no separate CAM bill, everything bundled into one %. Considered as a possible renegotiation direction with DSC (trades transparency/control for predictability — no more itemized NNN to question, but no visibility into what's spent either).
